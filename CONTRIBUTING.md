# Contributing

## Why we need your stories

This guide is built on real experience — things that actually happened to us. But our experience is limited to our setup, our tools, and our mistakes. Your experience can fill in the gaps.

If you've installed a local AI agent (Hermes or any other) and been through the process of configuring it, breaking it, fixing it, and making it useful — your story can help someone who's just starting out.

## What we're looking for

### Real stories
Did your agent do something unexpected? Did you hit a wall and find a way around it? Did a particular configuration trick make a big difference? Write it up in `06-real-stories/`. We want the failures as much as the successes — maybe more.

### Runbooks
Did you figure out a repeatable process for something — setting up memory, configuring providers, recovering from a crash? Write it as a step-by-step runbook. If we can verify it works, we'll include it.

### Corrections
Did we get something wrong? Is a command outdated? Is a concept explained badly? Open an issue or submit a pull request. We'd rather be corrected than be wrong.

### Prompts
Do you have a prompt that works well for configuring an agent, auditing memory, or doing setup? Add it to the relevant `prompts/` folder.

## How to contribute

1. **Fork the repo**
2. **Create a branch** for your contribution
3. **Write your content** following the tone and structure of existing files
4. **Test your prompts and commands** — make sure they actually work before submitting
5. **Open a pull request** with a clear description of what you're adding

## Writing guidelines

- **Plain language.** If a beginner can't follow it, it needs rewriting.
- **Show, don't tell.** Use real examples, real commands, real outcomes.
- **No hype.** Don't promise things work perfectly. If something is tricky, say so.
- **No fear.** Address risks honestly but don't dramatize them.
- **Test everything.** Every command and prompt must be verified before publishing.

## What we don't accept

- Content that includes API keys, passwords, or other secrets
- Content that promotes a specific paid product as an advertisement
- Content that's purely theoretical with no real-world backing
- Content that dismisses legitimate security concerns or mocks beginners for being cautious

## Review process

All pull requests are reviewed by Bill. We may:
- Accept as-is
- Request changes for clarity or accuracy
- Suggest moving content to a different section
- Decline if it doesn't fit the guide's purpose or quality bar

We aim to review within a week. We're not a big team — thanks for your patience.

## Questions?

Open an issue on the repo. We'll do our best to help.
