# Creators Worth Following

This section is not a popularity list.

It is a beginner filter.

When you are learning agents, there is too much content. Some of it is useful. Some of it is hype. Some of it assumes you already understand the words it is using.

The goal here is to help you find people who make agent work easier to understand.

## What makes a creator useful for beginners

Look for creators who:

- show real workflows, not just announcements
- explain what broke and how they fixed it
- share prompts, files, commands, or examples you can try
- talk about cost, safety, and maintenance
- make the work feel possible without pretending it is effortless
- distinguish demos from everyday use

Be careful with creators who:

- make every tool sound life-changing
- never show failures
- only talk about model rankings
- skip setup details
- imply that one prompt or one tool solves everything

## Suggested starting categories

Instead of following random AI news, start with categories:

- local agent setup
- command-line workflows
- coding agents
- personal knowledge bases
- automation for small businesses
- AI safety for everyday computer use
- open-source agent tools
- practical prompting

Those categories are more useful than chasing whatever is trending this week.

## Use your agent to find good people

Copy this prompt into your agent:

```text
Help me find practical creators to follow while I learn local AI agents.

I am a beginner.

I do not want hype or model-ranking drama.

Find creators who:
1. show real workflows
2. explain setup clearly
3. share prompts, files, commands, or examples
4. talk honestly about mistakes, cost, and maintenance
5. are useful for someone configuring their first agent

For each creator, tell me:
- name
- platform or website
- why they are useful
- one good starting video, article, or repo
- whether the content is beginner, intermediate, or advanced

Do not recommend someone unless you can explain why they fit.
```

## The beginner lesson

You do not need to follow everyone.

You need a small set of people who make you better at running your own setup.

Follow creators who reduce confusion.

Ignore creators who only increase excitement.
